Shadow AI is any AI tool used by employees without IT approval or oversight. It ranges from pasting client data into free ChatGPT, to browser extensions processing company emails, to approved SaaS platforms quietly adding AI features. IBM 2025: organizations with high Shadow AI exposure paid an average of $670,000 more per breach. This checklist helps you find out where you stand.

How to read this checklist: For most items, a NO answer indicates a gap that needs attention. Items marked with the warning indicator are reversed. For those items, a YES answer means a risk may already be active in your organization.

Section 1 of 6
Awareness
Do you know what is actually happening inside your organization?
High Impact
01
Do you and your IT team have clear visibility into which AI tools employees are using, including access through browsers and personal accounts?
02
Have you or any member of your leadership team used an AI tool that was not approved by your IT department?
Leadership Shadow AI use sets a precedent that undermines enforcement across the organization.
Section 2 of 6
Data Exposure
Do you know what is leaving your walls?
Critical Risk
03
Do your employees know which data must never be entered into AI tools, including passwords, API keys, client records, financial data, and HR files?
04
Are employees currently using free versions of AI tools (such as free ChatGPT) with company data?
Free-tier tools use your inputs to train their models. That data cannot be retrieved or deleted.
05
Are employees accessing AI tools through personal accounts rather than enterprise-licensed versions?
47% of employees using AI at work access it through personal accounts with no enterprise data agreements or audit trail (Netskope, 2026).
06
Do you know whether the SaaS platforms you already use (Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, Salesforce) have AI features enabled and properly configured?
Section 3 of 6
Policy and Governance
Do you have guardrails in place?
High Impact
07
Do you have a written AI Acceptable Use Policy that specifically defines which tools are approved, restricted, and prohibited?
08
Have your employees been trained on what Shadow AI is and why it creates risk for your business?
09
Is there a clear, simple process for employees to request approval of a new AI tool before using it?
Section 4 of 6
Compliance and Legal Exposure
Do you know what you are liable for?
Critical Risk
10
Do you know which data privacy regulations apply to your business, and do your AI vendors have signed Data Processing Agreements (DPAs) in place?
11
Can your IT team produce an audit trail of company data sent through AI tools, and would you know within 24 hours if a data exposure occurred?
Section 5 of 6
Monitoring and Detection
Can you actually see it?
High Impact
12
Does your IT team receive alerts when new or unauthorized AI tools are accessed on company networks or devices?
13
Are AI meeting assistants or transcription tools being used in client, HR, legal, or financial meetings without explicit approval or participant notification?
AI transcription bots can record and store client conversations, HR discussions, and confidential meetings with no controls or deletion rights.
14
Are browser extensions on company-managed devices being monitored for AI capabilities and data access permissions?
